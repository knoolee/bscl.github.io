Behavioral Signals of Cognition Lab

Digital phenotyping, speech markers, and multimodal detection of early cognitive change

Digital phenotyping, speech, and cognitive health

Behavioral and language markers of early cognitive change

Our research develops scalable approaches to detect early cognitive vulnerability using ambient sensing, speech and language analysis, and multimodal behavioral data. The work focuses on aging in place, functional change, and clinically meaningful indicators of Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias.

Research areas

This program sits at the intersection of nursing informatics, aging, behavioral sensing, and applied AI.

Digital phenotyping of daily function

We study how in-home activity patterns, mobility, and daily routines change over time in older adults, with emphasis on ecologically valid measures of independence and functional decline.

Ambient sensors ADLs Aging in place

Speech and language markers

We examine speech, discourse, and language-derived features that may reflect early cognitive vulnerability and support scalable screening and monitoring approaches.

Speech Discourse SLMs

Multimodal early detection

We aim to integrate digital behavioral measures with clinical and biomarker information to improve interpretation, validation, and eventual translational use in dementia research.
